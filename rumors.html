Recent Rumors

Where will Yoenis Cespedes end up this offseason?

Obviously the Mets really want to keep their star outfielder, but as you already know, Cespedes opted out of his contract. The Mets have offered him a qualifying offer, but its not likely that he will accept. Instead, he intends to try out the free agent market and see if he can land a four or five year deal. I still wouldn't count the Mets out because they could possibly construct a similar deal as they did last year. Giving him the years he wants but less money, and an opt out clause after a year or so. Its going to be interesting to see what Sandy Alderson can do with regards to Cespedes.

Relievers?

With a good number of relievers trying their luck on the free agent market, the Mets will be looking to restock their bullpen in preperation for the new season. We do have some solid pieces left, but we need to find the missing links. The Mets will probably be looking for a lefty specialist, and a couple of hard throwing right hand relievers. If they can fill those spots, they should be good with pitchers.

Position Players?

With a good number of players not being brought back, the Mets will need to plug some holes in the field. Even if Cespedes does not resign, we still have an abundance of outfielders. The problem is going to be more in the infield. I would say that the Mets would be looking at potential replacement at first base and the catcher position. There is a possibility that the Mets talk with outfielders, but since Bruce's option was picked up, the most work should be done on the infield.

What will Alderson be looking for in the offseason?

From what we can tell, the Mets will be looking at a variety of different pieces to help plug some holes. Relievers, first basemen, outfielder, and catcher, are all positions that the general manager will be taking a look at during this offseason. We will share more news on which players the Mets are looking at when negotiations begin.
