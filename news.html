Recent Updates

Is Yoenis Cespedes going to stay with the Mets?

Many reports indicate that after the World Series is over, Cespedes will opt out of his contract with the Mets and look for a new deal. Sources are being told that Cespedes is looking for a 3 or 4 year deal, and is looking for a lot of money. Unfortunately the Mets will most likely not have the money to spend on Yoenis Cespedes.

So what does this mean for their team now? Are they capable of repeating success without Cespedes' bat in their lineup? I guess those are all questions that we will find out during the offseason.

Will the starting rotation be healthy next year?

Last season the starting pitching was a mess. No one could stay healthy. First Harvey went down, then Wheeler never came up, and then deGrom and Matz went down towards the end of the season. The only ones left were Syndergaard and Colon. Yes, they did get some help from guys like Gsellman and Lugo, but they cant be counted on for this much work just yet. Hopefully this season our starting rotation can come back healthy. Good starting pitching goes a long way. If we have it, I say we have a good shot at making the playoffs for a third year in a row.

Can David Wright stay healthy?

Over the last two seasons, David Wright has not played more than 60 games. Obviously he had some serious injuries to deal with, however, how will this effect us going forward. Will he still be able to produce at a major league level? Can he play third base, or is it time to move him to another position? If he gets hurt again, is it time for him to retire? These are all questions that will hopefully be answered by the time spring training rolls around.

What is happening with Terry Collins

As of right now, Terry Collins is one of the oldest managers in the MLB. During the first few seasons that he was with the Mets, the team didn't do much, as they were still in the rebuilding phase. However, the last two seasons under Collins have been great. Making it to the playoffs twice, and almost winning the World Series in 2015, will probably be the highlights of Terry Collins' managerial carrer. There are reports coming out stating that COllins is done after the 2017 season. Do you think the Mets have it in them to bring home a championship under Collins?
